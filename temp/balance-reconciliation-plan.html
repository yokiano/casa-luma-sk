Shop Balance & Reconciliation Plan

A practical plan for knowing how much money the business should have, then checking it against KBiz / Kasikorn bank, cash counts, and Loyverse POS.

1. Expected balance

The ledger says what should exist

Every sale, deposit, expense, salary, fee, transfer, and owner movement is recorded into accounts.

2. Real-world evidence

POS, slips, bank, cash count

Loyverse, scan slips, credit settlements, cash drawer counts, and KBiz statements prove what really happened.

3. Reconciliation

Differences must have a reason

If expected and actual do not match, the gap becomes a task: missing expense, pending card settlement, cash not deposited, fee, mistake, or timing delay.

The core formula

Ledger / book balance what we should have
↔
Actual balance KBiz + counted cash + pending settlements

The goal is not that every source matches every day. The goal is that every difference is understood and assigned to a bucket.

Income flow by payment method

1

Sale happens

Cashier selects Cash, Scan/QR, or Credit Card in Loyverse.

2

Loyverse daily report

Shows total sales split by payment method, including refunds/voids.

3

Record into ledger

Cash goes to Cash on Hand. Scan/card goes to Clearing/Pending Settlement.

4

Money arrives

Cash counted/deposited. Scan/card appears in KBiz, sometimes net of fees.

5

Reconcile

Match expected money to actual money. Explain any difference.

Cash sales

Record immediately

Cash increases the drawer. Later deposit is only a transfer from cash to bank.

Watch for: cash expenses, salaries, missing cash, change/floats.

Scan / QR sales

Use clearing account

Sale is recorded today, but bank deposit may appear later or grouped with other sales.

Watch for: wrong method in POS, delayed deposits, fees.

Credit card

Gross vs net

Loyverse may show gross sale. Bank may receive net after card fees.

Record: sales gross, bank deposit net, fee as expense.

Expenses

Payment source matters

Same expense category, different money source: bank scan, cash, card, owner paid.

Always record: amount, date, category, proof, paid from where.

Daily / weekly rhythm

Daily close

  • Export/check Loyverse sales by payment method
  • Count cash drawer and record cash difference
  • Record scan-phone expense slips
  • Record cash expenses and cash salaries paid that day
  • Flag unknown or suspicious payments immediately
  • Keep receipt/slip photos linked to records

Weekly / monthly reconciliation

  • Import KBiz statement
  • Match bank deposits to scan/card clearing
  • Match bank withdrawals to expense records
  • Add missing bank fees and automatic payments
  • Review owner withdrawals/injections
  • Produce difference report: explained vs unexplained

Easy mental model to remember

Sale

What did we earn, and by which payment method?

Source

Where did the money go first: cash drawer, bank, or pending clearing?

Spend

What did we pay, and from which source: bank scan, cash, card, owner?

Match

Can every bank/cash movement be matched to a ledger record?

Minimum accounts to track

  • Kasikorn / KBiz Bank — actual bank account
  • Cash on Hand — shop drawer / safe
  • Scan/QR Clearing — scan sales expected but not reconciled
  • Credit Card Clearing — card sales expected but not settled
  • Sales Revenue — gross income from Loyverse
  • Expense categories — rent, supplies, salary, utilities, fees, etc.
  • Owner / Loans / Tax — money that is not normal sale/expense

Common forgotten things

  • Bank fees and card processing fees
  • Refunds, chargebacks, voids, discounts
  • Cash taken for small purchases
  • Cash salaries or advances
  • Owner pays a company expense personally
  • Owner withdraws money for personal use
  • Supplier credit / unpaid bills
  • Deposits that combine several days of sales
  • Money in transit: sale happened but bank has not received it yet
